# Project proposal

Restaurant operators deciding where to open a halal concept in New York City face a classic information problem: Yelp and generic foot-traffic dashboards show “busy places,” but they do little to separate neighborhoods where diners already talk about halal from neighborhoods where kitchens licensed as halal-relevant cuisines cluster, or where inspections suggest a brittle operating environment. We work at the Neighborhood Tabulation Area (NTA) level and combine Yelp review text jointly labeled by Gemini (`data/raw/yelp_reviews_with_zones.csv`, `data/raw/gemini_labels_full.csv`), NYC DOHMH restaurant universe and cuisine classifications from CAMIS-style hygiene extracts (`data/raw/restaurant_hygiene.csv`), and inspection-grade histories aggregated to NTAs (`data/processed/inspections.parquet`). The motivating user-facing question is: **given public signals of halal curiosity, culinary supply shape, sanitation context, and change over recent years, which NTAs merit a prioritized short-list for halal ventures?** The problem is compelling because NYC’s zoning and rent dynamics make location mistakes costly, while curated halal-focused evidence at neighborhood granularity is scarce for independent proprietors compared with chain-caliber proprietary models.

Technically we use **k-means clustering** (implemented explicitly in NumPy in `src/halal_kmeans.py`) together with inertia and silhouette analysis (see `scripts/run_phase1.py`) to segment neighborhoods into interpretable halal-market archetypes driven by normalized demand-, supply-, and gap-style features; these clusters ground every downstream recommendation and the Streamlit experience. Rankings fuse demand, heuristic supply–demand gap, and viability heuristics in `scripts/run_phase2.py`, augmented by cosine-similarity neighbors (`src/halal_similarity.py`) so operators can scout analog NTAs quickly. Phase three layers **Gaussian mixture models** with BIC-guided component counts for soft-assignment risk mixtures (`GaussianMixture` in `src/halal_risk.py`) and applies **ridge regression** (`Ridge`) with **k-fold cross-validation** (`KFold`) in `src/halal_forecast.py` (orchestrated via `scripts/run_phase3.py`) to score forward-looking proxies for sustained halal discussion and new-entry counts. Together, clustering and mixture modeling capture heterogeneous neighborhood geometry; regularized regression with CV stabilizes moderately sized longitudinal fits; cosine similarity supports merchant-facing pairwise exploration; weighted linear composites keep ranking logic auditable—all surfaced through Streamlit (`frontend/app.py`).
